The Search Intelligence Layer

Multi-signal retrieval with weighted rank fusion so every match is grounded, explainable, and actionable.

Precise Matching

Structured field-level correlation that catches exact and near-exact alignments across canonicalized skills, proficiency scores, and resolved role archetypes.

Catches → exact titles, canonical skills, credentials

Relevance Scoring

Statistical term-frequency analysis across enriched Golden Records. Finds strong keyword signals even when profile structure varies.

Catches → keyword-rich profiles, domain terminology

Neural Embeddings

Dense vector representations that capture conceptual similarity. Understands that roles and skills can be equivalent without sharing a single word.

Catches → equivalent roles, adjacent skills, latent meaning

Learned Sparse Expansion

ML-derived token expansion that broadens recall by inferring terms a candidate would use — even if they don't appear in their profile.

Catches → implicit skills, inferred experience, semantic gaps

Why This Matters
No single search method captures every type of match. Exact matching misses equivalent roles. Semantic search misses precise credentials. We use all of them.
The fusion is weighted — not equal blending. The system dynamically adjusts which signals matter most depending on the nature of each query.
Vectors don't search raw text — they search through canonicalized skills, resolved role archetypes, proficiency scores, and career trajectories. That's our enriched intelligence layer.

Speaker Notes

Now let's talk about what happens when you actually search across this enriched data. We don't use one search algorithm — we use four fundamentally different approaches, each designed to catch a different type of match. Precise matching catches exact alignments across our canonical skills and role archetypes. Relevance scoring finds strong keyword signals across our Golden Records. Neural embeddings understand conceptual similarity — that an SRE and a DevOps Engineer are the same thing even though they share no words. And learned sparse expansion infers terms a candidate would use, even if they haven't listed them. These four signals are fused using a weighted rank fusion engine — not a simple average, but a context-sensitive weighting. And critically, these vectors aren't searching raw text. They're searching through the enriched data layers we've been building — the skills taxonomy, the role archetypes, proficiency scores, career trajectories. That combination of multiple search methods with our proprietary data enrichment is what makes this defensible.
