Speaker Notes

Now I want to show you what's underneath all of these capabilities — the infrastructure that makes recommendations, pathways, and search possible. We build a weighted relationship graph through custom aggregations across our entire dataset. Not a theoretical model — a computed graph where every edge represents real co-occurrence strength across millions of profiles. The graph connects every attribute dimension to every other dimension. Skills to skills — we know that Python and Machine Learning co-occur with a weight of 0.91, meaning they almost always appear together. But it goes deeper. Titles to skills — we know which skills statistically define each role. Titles to titles — we know which roles are adjacent and which career transitions are common. Skills to industries — we know how skill demand varies by sector. The power is in multi-hop traversal. When you ask about a "Product Analyst in Fintech," the first hop gives you the direct skills: SQL, A/B Testing, Python. The second hop reveals adjacent titles that share those skill signatures: Growth Analyst, BI Analyst. The third hop follows the industry edges and surfaces something no keyword search would ever find: that Risk Analytics in Payments is a viable lateral path with a surprising skill overlap. This graph is what powers everything. Title recommendations, skill inference, career pathways, gap analysis, search ranking — they all traverse these weighted edges. And because the graph is built from our proprietary dataset, it can't be replicated with generic data.
