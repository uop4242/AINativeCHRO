The World's Talent Data Is Broken.
We Fixed It.

For close to a decade, Censia has built and continues to build the domain intelligence to make sense of the world's messiest data: talent data. We track and contextualize over 550M professionals, but the real value is what we've learned. How roles evolve. How responsibilities overlap across industries. What a title or skill actually means in context, not just today, but over the past decade. As AI reshapes every role in every industry, that understanding is what separates useful output from noise.

The Data Problem

Fragmented, inconsistent, unreliable talent data across every source. Resumes, job posts, HR systems, the open web. The complexity in this domain defeats general-purpose approaches.

The AI Imperative

Understanding how to upskill and reskill people as AI reshapes roles, industries, and the nature of work itself.

The Intelligence Layer

Domain-specific models that structure and enrich talent data. The preprocessing that makes downstream AI produce results you can trust.

The Foundation

Powers certifications, career pathways, workforce planning, and precision talent matching at scale.

Speaker Notes

Talent data is the messiest data on the planet. Fragmented across millions of sources, riddled with inconsistencies. Profiles are everywhere. The hard part is understanding what they actually mean. What a role required 8 years ago versus today. How responsibilities shift and overlap across companies and industries. We spent 8+ years building the domain-specific models to structure this: canonicalizing skills, resolving titles, scoring proficiency, tracking how the talent landscape evolves over time. Now, as AI reshapes every industry, that deep domain intelligence becomes the foundation for everything from workforce planning to precision matching.
