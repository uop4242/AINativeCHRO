Speaker Notes

Here's where our skills graph and career intelligence combine. Take a Data Analyst who wants to become a Data Scientist. The proficiency model shows that 4 of 5 core skills transfer directly — SQL, Python basics, statistics, visualization. We can quantify exactly how much development each skill needs based on proficiency distance. But now layer in the career intelligence: we've analyzed millions of career histories and know that Data Analyst to Data Scientist is a high-frequency transition with well-established patterns. We can tell you the typical tenure before the move, what skills the successful transitioners had, and what other roles are even closer to this person's current profile. And through our role archetypes, we identify adjacent opportunities — ML Engineer, Analytics Lead — that this person might not have considered but is well-positioned for. This isn't generic career advice. It's personalized pathways built on real data.
