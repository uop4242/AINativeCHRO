The Living Intelligence Layer

The challenge Censia solves is the underlying data infrastructure: resolving entity ambiguity at scale (for example, “Is J. Smith, Software Engineer in Mountain View the same person as John Smith, Engineer at Google?”), maintaining accuracy across hundreds of millions of records, and compounding that intelligence over time. Censia has been doing exactly this since 2017, and that depth of structured, clean workforce data is not something that can be replicated in a few engineering sprints.

Speaker Notes

Now let me show you where we're headed next. On top of that pipeline, we're building a living intelligence layer — this is the next evolution of our platform. First, Role Archetypes. We use ML fingerprinting and clustering to group similar job titles together. DevOps Engineer, Site Reliability Engineer, Platform Engineer — these are all the same archetype. This means when you search for DevOps talent, you automatically get SREs and Platform Engineers too, without having to guess every title variation. Second, Career Transitions. We analyze hundreds of millions of career histories to map who moves from what role to what role, how often, and how long they typically stay before transitioning. We can see that Backend Engineers frequently transition into DevOps roles, and we know the typical tenure before that move. Third, Skill Inference. Through statistical lift analysis, we determine which skills are strongly associated with which roles. Kubernetes is 8.5 times more common among DevOps professionals than the general population — that's a powerful signal. Fourth, Career Readiness Scores. We pre-compute a readiness score for each person against various target roles, combining transition likelihood, tenure, skills overlap, and career trajectory. One number that tells you how ready someone is for a role they've never held. Fifth, Archetype Resolution. Users just type naturally — "Platform Engineer" — and the system resolves it to the right archetype through exact match, fuzzy match, or embedding similarity. Here's the key unlock: look at the flow on the right. Take a Backend Engineer with 3.5 years of experience. Our transition analysis shows people like them frequently become DevOps Engineers. They show a high readiness signal for DevOps roles. We surface this person as hidden talent — someone who has never held a DevOps title but is statistically likely to succeed in the role. This is what we mean by finding people who COULD do a job, not just who HAS done it. And crucially, this intelligence layer is living — it continuously re-derives and reindexes as new data comes in.
