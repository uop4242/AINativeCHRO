Speaker Notes

Now let's talk about what happens when you take that attribute graph and turn it into a recommender. This is our most advanced recommendation engine. Rather than relying on simple lookups or manually curated association tables, we traverse the weighted graph to generate recommendations dynamically. Here's how it works: you give the system an input — a title, a skill, any attribute — and it walks the graph, aggregating signal strength across connected edges. The output is a confidence-ranked list of recommendations. For "Machine Learning Engineer," the graph surfaces PyTorch and TensorFlow at 0.93 — almost always co-occurring. Deep Learning at 0.89. But it also recommends titles: Data Scientist, Applied Scientist, AI Platform Engineer — roles that share deep structural overlap in the graph. What makes this different from a standard recommender? Three things. First, it's computed, not curated — every recommendation is derived from statistical aggregation across millions of profiles, not a hand-built mapping. Second, it's multi-dimensional — the same traversal engine works across skills to titles, titles to skills, skills to industries, any attribute pair. Third, it's confidence-ranked. Weak associations are automatically pruned. Only statistically significant connections surface. This powers everything from job description autofill to candidate skill inference to career pathway generation.
