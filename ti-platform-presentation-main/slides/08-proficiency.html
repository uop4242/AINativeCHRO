Speaker Notes

This is where it gets powerful. We don't guess at proficiency — we model it mathematically. Three indices — Recency, Maturity, and Depth — each follow their own mathematical curve. Recency uses exponential decay: a skill you used last month scores high, one from three years ago fades. Maturity follows a saturation curve: your first year of experience matters most, then diminishing returns. Depth accumulates across your entire career, weighted by how central the skill was to each role. These aren't just added together — they're weighted and combined with a staleness penalty that continuously down-weights dormant skills. And we're multi-role aware: if you're actively using Python today, your score doesn't get dragged down because you also used it in a junior role eight years ago. We use a max-blend approach that favors your best recent evidence. The result: a defensible 1-to-5 proficiency score for every skill on every person. And we can show distributions at any level. We're also exploring LMS integration — giving a calibrated boost when someone completes serious training, without letting a quick online course inflate their score. Every parameter is tunable per customer — weights, half-lives, thresholds, staleness curves. We can recalculate instantly with different settings.
